The Cuisine Experience

Tropical

Old friends, new friends, happy faces, rum cocktails in hand, in the breathtaking crystalline waters of Île Plate (map) on the north coast of Mauritius, an island nation in the Indian Ocean. Everyone has visions of what paradise is like, most of which involve tropical islands with white sandy beaches, palms swaying in the soft breeze and crystal-clear water. Mauritius certainly ticked all the boxes but a place can only be paradise if the experience is shared with a loved one or good friends.

Arctic

Feel the Arctic cold on your face, be active to keep warm, savour a drink from a glass made of ice and witness the mesmerising dance of the Northern Lights. Experience all forms of snow and winter with all your senses, holistically and easily with us, with all the services you need nearby!

In the renowned Ice Restaurant of Arctic SnowHotel (map) offers you tasty dinners whether you are just visiting the restaurant or a guest staying at the hotel.

Historic

While many people would relish a table overlooking the iconic Lake Bled Castle (map), having a delicious dinner or lunch from inside the castle is even better! Built in 1011, Bled Castle has been lovingly restored and holds a museum of Slavic history, exploring the tumultuous story that the castle holds over the course of 1000 years. With its vantage point from the top of the precarious cliff overlooking Bled, the lake and the mountains beyond, it’s understandable why anyone would want to build their castle in such a beautiful location.

Top of the World

Sitting at 3080m above the sea level, you’ll certainly be leaving this restaurant on a high...! The Top Mountain Star restaurant in Obergurgl (map) is technically a bar that offers up some delicious snacks and small meals, but we all know that the best part of the meal at this fantastic restaurant is those views. Described as an architectural masterpiece, from its position perched on a narrow ridge of the Wurmkogel the Top Mountain Star offers a 360 degree panoramic view of the surrounding peaks and valleys of the Ötz.
